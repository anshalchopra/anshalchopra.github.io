My Story

Welcome to my portfolio. If you're here, you're probably looking for someone who's good with analytics, and I am. But that's not really what this page is about.

Somewhere along the way, most of our connections moved online. Somewhere along the way, we found out you can't actually build something real behind a screen, no matter how good the interface is. So before you scroll through case studies and dashboards, I wanted to tell you how I actually got here. Not the resume version. The real one.

See more Show less

I grew up in a business household, middle-class, nothing fancy. But numbers were always around me: revenue, margins, inventory, the language my family spoke without meaning to teach it. I picked it up anyway. I was the kid who wanted to know why something made money, not just how much.

But that wasn't the plan for me. My dad wanted a pilot in the family, so a pilot I trained to become. Early mornings before school. Discipline drilled in before I understood why it mattered. Communication, problem-solving, staying calm and analytical when things go wrong at 30,000 feet. I didn't know it yet, but I was being built for something. I just assumed it was a cockpit.

Then, right when it was time to actually apply, a medical condition ruled me out. Just like that. Years of training, gone in a single conversation. I remember the strange quiet after, not devastation exactly, more like standing in a room where all the furniture had just been removed. I had no backup plan, because I'd never let myself need one.

Not long after, I moved to Canada. New country, no plan, and a mind that kept circling back to a version of myself that no longer existed.

What followed wasn't a straight line. It rarely is. Mechatronics, because it felt close to what I'd trained for. Computer science, because it felt more useful. Environmental science, because I was still searching for a "why." None of them were wrong, exactly. None of them were quite it, either.

Then a friend mentioned data science, almost in passing, the kind of comment that doesn't feel important until, months later, you realize it changed everything. And there it was: the curiosity from those childhood dinner-table conversations, the discipline from years of training for a career I never got to have, the calm-under-pressure thinking that was supposed to keep a plane in the air. All of it had actually been preparing me for this the whole time. I just hadn't known what "this" was yet.

That's the part I want you to take from this page, more than any chart or dashboard: behind every analysis is a person who got there in a roundabout, occasionally painful, deeply human way. That's what I think we lose when everything moves online, not the information, but the story behind it. So if you're here for the analytics, you're in good hands. But I'm hoping you leave remembering the person behind them, too.
